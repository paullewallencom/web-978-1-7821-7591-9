Mono Lake

The most unusual views in the Eastern Sierra you get to see as soon as you come down Tioga Pass towards Lee Vining. Right before the junction with US 395 is a vista point overlooking Mono Lake. The water of most of the inlets of this lake go into the L.A. aqueduct. As a result the water level of this very alkaline lake is a lot lower than before, exposing the crystalline structures that developed below the lake surface, called tufas. Five miles south and then five miles east on SR 120 is the South Tufa area, the best spot for viewing and photography. Click on a photograph for more details.
